Alternative forms[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Old High German ouga, from Proto-Germanic *augô, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₃ekʷ- (“eye; to see”).

Noun[edit]

Auge n (genitive Auges, plural Augen, diminutive Äugelchen n or Äuglein n)

  1. eye
  2. germ, bud
  3. (on dice) spot
