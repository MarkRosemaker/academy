Dutch[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Middle Dutch betrachten (“to look at, to observe”), from Old Dutch *bitrahton. Equivalent to be- +‎ trachten.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /bəˈtrɑxtə(n)/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: be‧trach‧ten
  • Rhymes: -ɑxtən

Verb[edit]

betrachten

  1. (transitive) to exercise or consider carefully
  2. (Belgium) to pursue
    Synonyms: nastreven, beogen

Usage notes[edit]

The verb in the meaning "to exercise carefully" is limited to a number of expressions, like:

  • zijn plicht betrachten - acquit oneself of one's duty
  • de nodige zorgvuldigheid betrachten - exercise due care

German[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Middle High German betrahten from Old High German bitrahtōn. Equivalent to be- +‎ trachten

Pronunciation[edit]

  • Audio
    		(file)
  • IPA(key): /bəˈtʁaχtən/, [bəˈtʁaχtn̩]

Verb[edit]

betrachten (third-person singular simple present betrachtet, past tense betrachtete, past participle betrachtet, auxiliary haben)

  1. to look (at)
  2. to look, to consider (at)
  3. to regard, to consider, look upon (something)
