German[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Middle High German iuch, from Old High German iuwih (compare Old English ēowiċ), from West Germanic *iwwiz, variant of Proto-Germanic *izwiz, dative/accusative of *jūz, from Proto-Indo-European *yū́. Compare Dutch jou, u, Low German jo, ju, jug, West Frisian jo, English you.
