German[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Middle High German enpfelhen, enphelhen, entfelhen, intfelhen, from Old High German intfelhan, intfelahan, from int- +‎ + felhan (“to preserve; entrust; confide in; surrender”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ʔɛmˈpfeːlən/
  • Audio
    		(file)

Verb[edit]

empfehlen (class 4 strong, third-person singular simple present empfiehlt, past tense empfahl, past participle empfohlen, auxiliary haben)

  1. (transitive) to recommend; to advocate
  2. (reflexive) to offer one's services
